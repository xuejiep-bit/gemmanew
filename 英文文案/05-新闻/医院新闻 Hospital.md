Big news from Shenzhen recently — Phase II of the Longgang No. 6 People's Hospital officially opened on 16 September. Throughout the hospital's journey from planning to opening, the flooring material — in constant daily contact with patients and staff — went through layer after layer of rigorous screening. GEMMANEW flooring stood out in the early stages of the Longgang No. 6 Hospital project on the strength of solid quality and a comprehensive testing system. The story of that "quality breakout" begins three years earlier, with a surprise inspection challenge.

In 2022, construction at the Shenzhen Longgang No. 4 People's Hospital project was proceeding smoothly when it was abruptly halted by spot-check results on a batch of PVC flooring. Surprisingly, the failing product came from a well-known international flooring brand — it did not pass the rigorous on-site witnessed sampling conducted by the Longgang Construction Works Bureau. Even more shocking was the gulf between spot-check data and the submitted approval documents. The commissioned inspection report the brand had previously submitted showed a product weight of 2,980 g/m², abrasion loss of 1.1 mm³, and a T-grade wear rating; Yet on-site sampling measured a weight of 3,498 g/m², abrasion loss of 4.8 mm³, and only an M-grade wear rating — falling far short of the T-grade standard mandated by the Shenzhen Construction Works Bureau's tender technical parameters. The final verdict — "Report does not match the actual product" — settled the matter. The client immediately revoked the supplier's qualification, the nearly 2,000 m² already installed was torn out, and the project ground to a halt. With the schedule under pressure, the project urgently needed a "reliable partner" to step in and rescue it. GEMMANEW soon entered the bidding round alongside three overseas brands. In the end, GEMMANEW won the tender on the strength of better value for money, swiftly submitted a technical commitment letter to the latest national standard, and saw its approval documents pass scrutiny by both the main contractor and the supervisor. But the Longgang Construction Works Bureau did not relax its requirements for the emergency replacement — instead, it set up an even stricter "four-gate" challenge to put GEMMANEW's product through a full examination: Gate 1 — Production traceability. The supervisor and main contractor's leads travelled directly to the GEMMANEW factory, randomly inspected products on the production line, and oversaw every step from raw-material ratios to vulcanisation, ensuring that the "actual product" matched the "reported data" exactly. Gate 2 — On-site sampling. The day the product arrived on site, the Construction Works Bureau, supervisor, main contractor, and others jointly witnessed the unpacking, sampling, and sign-off — an open and transparent process. Gate 3 — Inter-provincial testing. Samples were escorted in person by air to the CTC testing laboratory in Beijing. The verdict — "All tested items pass" — saw GEMMANEW flooring clear the most critical hurdle. But the test was not yet over. Gate 4 followed without pause: as the project approached completion, the supervisor launched a final round of sampling and inspection. This time samples were split between two authoritative bodies — one to the Shenzhen Academy of Building Research for dedicated TVOC emission testing, and the other to SGS for comprehensive verification of the product's physical and chemical stability. Dual-track testing, double assurance — GEMMANEW flooring once again passed the ultimate exam with every line of results meeting the standard. On 5 September 2023, the Longgang No. 4 People's Hospital project was successfully completed. GEMMANEW flooring delivered flawlessly, clearing all four levels of quality inspection.

The project explicitly required all PVC homogeneous through-body sheet flooring to strictly comply with the Shenzhen Construction Works Bureau's 2019–2021 strategic-procurement technical standard — the "quality ceiling" for public projects in Shenzhen. Three internationally known imported flooring brands joined GEMMANEW in the bidding. In the end, GEMMANEW broke through the field of three imported competitors — not on price alone, but on the technical fluency it showed throughout early discussions and the quality reputation built up project after project. The client never let up on quality, and GEMMANEW fully cooperated with every inspection procedure. During the brand approval stage, GEMMANEW had already submitted the required [Type Inspection Report] to confirm that the product met access standards. Once materials arrived on site, the supervisor and main contractor went further: every product also had to be sampled on site and sent to an authoritative testing centre for full inspection. On the day the materials finally arrived, the Longgang Construction Works Bureau, the supervisor, the main contractor, and others were all on site to witness the sampling process from start to finish. Every sample was sealed and labelled to ensure fairness and transparency. Once sealed, the samples were escorted in person by air directly to the CTC (China Testing &amp; Certification) laboratory in Beijing. Waiting for results is often agonising, but the GEMMANEW team remained calm and confident throughout. From raw-material traceability to process control, from wear values to the stability of physical and chemical performance, every detail has been honed and proven — more than enough to withstand the strictest scrutiny. The inspection report duly arrived. Compared rigorously against the "Shenzhen Construction Works Bureau 2019–2021 Technical Parameters for Homogeneous Through-Body PVC Sheet Flooring," GEMMANEW's PVC homogeneous flooring measured 2,832 g/m² in total weight per unit area — better than the ≤ 2,850 g/m² standard; wear class T with a precisely controlled wear value of 1.9; longitudinal and transverse heated dimensional change rates of 0.06 (X) and 0.08 (Y), far below the ≤ 0.4 limit; and a residual indentation of just 0.02, showing outstanding dimensional stability.
